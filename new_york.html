New York

Attractions

Broadway

Went to see Wicked, which was amazing on Broadway.

Central Park

Spent park of a day here to read and eat our bagels.

Grand Central Station

An architecturally beautiful building that holds so much history.

9/11 Memorial

One of the best museums I've ever been to. It moved me to tears.

Grand Bazaar

A giant flea market at the Upper West Side

Times Square

Our hotel was right near it, so we went here frequently. It truly is the city that never sleeps.

The MET

The iconic MET steps where we stopped to take pictures then went to museum.

Washington Square Arch

We met a 92 year old woman who was a UofM graduate as well.

Food

Carbonara

The best carbonara I've ever had. It was a perfect mix of everything

Dumplings

One of Chinatown's specialties. These all were $5.

Lenwich

I'm not even kidding when I say this sandwich is my background.

Lox Bagel

I'm a huge lox fan, so having it on a NYC bagel was to die for.

Pork Buns

Carmelized pork buns at Chinatown that costs $1 each.

Pizza

Your typical NYC pizza. So yummy.

Matcha Coffee

Went to a flower cafe and got the cutests drinks.

Serendipity

From the famous movie. It was frozen hot chocolate, but super expensive.
